# Hult Prize Business Pitch

Use this skill for:
- pitch deck outlines
- slide-by-slide review
- judging-criteria alignment
- business model storytelling
- impact narrative
- market sizing
- founder script and speaking notes
- likely Q&A preparation

## Primary goal

Help the team communicate a credible, scalable, impact-driven venture
that feels competition-ready rather than classroom-theoretical.

## Default pitch structure

Use this slide order unless the user already has a deck:

1. Title
2. Problem
3. Why now
4. Solution
5. Product or MVP
6. Market
7. Business model
8. Go-to-market
9. Competition and differentiation
10. Impact model
11. Traction or validation
12. Team
13. Ask / next milestone

## What to optimize for

Every pitch should be judged on four things:
- clarity
- credibility
- scalability
- impact

## Workflow

1. Identify the startup type and beneficiary.
2. Clarify the problem in one sentence.
3. Distinguish beneficiary, user, customer, and payer.
4. Define the wedge:
   - why this entry point
   - why this team
   - why now
5. Make sure business model and impact model are both present.
6. Remove vague claims and replace them with evidence or assumptions.
7. Build the narrative so each slide earns the next one.

## Slide rules

### Problem slide
- quantify the pain if possible
- use one user group first
- avoid broad global statements unless backed up

### Solution slide
- explain what the product actually does
- show workflow, not slogans
- make the mechanism obvious

### MVP slide
- show what exists today
- if early stage, show prototype, pilot, user interviews, waitlist, LOIs, or operational test

### Market slide
- prefer bottom-up market logic
- avoid inflated TAM-only storytelling

### Business model slide
- state exactly who pays, how much, and how often
- include unit economics if available

### Impact slide
- define measurable outcomes
- distinguish outputs from outcomes
- say how impact will be measured

### Competition slide
- show the real alternatives, including offline behavior and "do nothing"
- explain differentiation in operational terms, not adjectives

### Team slide
- prove execution fitness
- highlight founder-market fit, operational access, technical ability, or trust advantage

## Review format

When reviewing a pitch, use this format for each weak area:
- issue
- why it matters
- specific rewrite or fix

If reviewing slides, score each one on:
- clarity /10
- credibility /10
- persuasion /10

## Common problems to catch

- problem is too broad
- impact is inspiring but business model is weak
- business model exists but impact path is vague
- TAM is huge but beachhead is undefined
- no proof anyone cares
- deck uses too many words and not enough logic
- competition slide ignores incumbent behavior
- team slide lists titles instead of advantages
- ask slide is unclear

## Q&A prep

Always prepare answers for:
- why this problem
- why you
- why now
- why won't incumbents win
- who pays
- what happens if adoption is slow
- how impact is measured
- what proof exists beyond interviews

## Tone

Push for sharp, investor-like clarity while keeping the Hult Prize
social impact lens intact.

Avoid:
- buzzwords
- vague optimism
- "AI/blockchain/app" as the core argument

Prefer:
- evidence
- tradeoffs
- numbers
- specific user behavior
- crisp storytelling
